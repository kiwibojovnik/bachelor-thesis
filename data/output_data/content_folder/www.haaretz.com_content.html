Five Films You Have to See at Tel Aviv's International LGBT Film Festival

Documentaries on Montgomery Clift, drag queens and transgender teens are as thrilling as features exploring the lives of members of the community

Nirit Anderman

The Tel Aviv International LGBT Film Festival, which opened Wednesday at the Tel Aviv Cinematheque, has survived despite obstacles imposed by the Culture Ministry, the city and the Cinematheque. As it has every year, TLVFest offers parties, panel discussions and meetings with artists. The main attraction, of course, is the wide variety of films that address LGBT issues, with LGBT actors in lead roles. Some of the movies are better than others; some engage with the local community, while others communicate stories of the LGBT experience in different places of the world.
