What are some pros and cons of free web hosting?

Pro: This may be obvious, but a major pro is that it doesn’t cost you a thing. This is particularly beneficial if funds are limited or if you’re not totally sure whether you actually need a site.

Pro: You can “get your feet wet” and learn a little bit about websites and what it takes to create them before you have the need for a more advanced (and often more expensive) site.

Con: You’re often limited in what you can do with your site due to storage restrictions and all of the other additional features that cost extra to add.

Con: Your website’s performance may be hindered with free web hosting options, potentially impacting your ability to build and retain loyal customers if they find it difficult to access or use.

What is shared hosting, cloud hosting, and WordPress hosting?

When looking at web hosting providers—whether free or paid—it’s not uncommon to come across terms like “shared hosting,” “cloud hosting,” and “WordPress hosting.” Let’s go over what each one means.

Shared hosting means that the web host will put many different websites’ data all on one server. This is generally the cheapest hosting option. However, if another site uses more storage or bandwidth, it can really slow your site down.

A site that is cloud hosted means that the hosting company uses multiple servers to store all its users’ important data. This offers the benefit of never paying for more resources than you need. Additionally, the data tends to be more secure and less likely to be impacted by a server that could potentially crash.

WordPress hosting refers to hosting that is provided specifically for WordPress sites. If your goal is to maximize speed and performance on that platform only, choosing a provider with this option may be the way to go.

Why is uptime important?

Have you ever visited a website only to receive an error message that it was temporarily down or not up at all? And did you go back and try it again later? Probably not, unless it was a major site that you frequently visit.

If your website is not up and running 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, you could be losing out on important leads. That’s why you want a web host provider that has high uptime percentages.

When your website is down, you’re losing money—now and in the long run.

Can I transfer my existing website to one of these web hosts?

Yes. If you don’t like your current web host provider and want to switch to another, you can totally do that.

However, it may cost you because not all sites offer this service for free. In fact, depending on the one you choose, you may spend a few hundred dollars (or more) if you decide to make a switch.
